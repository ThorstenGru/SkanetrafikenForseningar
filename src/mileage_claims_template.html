This page is about foreseeability, not just a confirmed delay. §4's own wording is "you are (or risk being) at least 20 minutes late... and choose to drive yourself instead" — a test of what was already knowable before boarding, not something only measurable after the fact. A journey where the delay only became apparent once already underway is §3 territory (price deduction for enduring it) — the rider never had the chance to decide to drive instead. So every row here has both a confirmed bad outcome and a real, matched disruption notice that was already active before the trip's own scheduled departure. compensation.html and claims.html show every network-wide delay illustratively, including ones that don't meet this bar — this page deliberately shows far fewer. Still not a filed application — verify each one against the Skånetrafiken app before submitting. Claim tracking here is the exact same claim_tracking record claims.html uses — marking one claimed on either page reflects on both. See COMPENSATION_RULES.md.
