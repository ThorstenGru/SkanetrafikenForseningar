Illustrative only, not an application. This page builds on the same network-wide delay data as compensation.html — it doesn't know which trips were actually yours. Its only job is to check internal consistency: if you tick a set of claims, do they form one continuous, physically possible day of travel? A real claim is for a journey you actually made — Skånetrafiken investigates every application individually and can require further proof for unusual or frequent claims (see docs/COMPENSATION_RULES.md §7).

Sources: Sommarbiljetten, docs/COMPENSATION_RULES.md (retrieved 2026-07-05). Connections are place + time only (straight-line distance between stops, no routing API) — a match means the two stops are close enough to plausibly be the same interchange point, not that a specific connecting service was verified. A trip only "confirms" its destination when it actually reached its own final stop (status Delayed); partially cancelled trips and trips whose final stop was never captured in the feed can start a chain but can't be relied on to extend one, since where the rider actually ended up isn't known.
